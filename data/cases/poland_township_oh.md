## Geological Setting

Poland Township is located in Mahoning County, northeastern Ohio — the same general region as the Youngstown disposal-induced seismicity case. The geological setting includes Paleozoic sedimentary rocks and Precambrian crystalline basement. The Utica Shale, a deep organic-rich formation, is the hydraulic fracturing target in this area.

## Industrial Operations

Horizontal hydraulic fracturing targeting the Utica Shale was conducted at a well in Poland Township in 2014. The Utica is at approximately 2–3 km depth, in close proximity to or in contact with basement rocks in this part of Ohio.

## Earthquake Sequence

A cluster of felt seismic events occurred during hydraulic fracturing operations in March 2014, with the largest reaching M2.1. The events were felt by local residents and were among the first documented cases of hydraulic fracturing-induced seismicity in Ohio. The events prompted the Ohio Department of Natural Resources to implement a traffic-light protocol for hydraulic fracturing operations in the state.

## Attribution in the Published Literature

Skoumal et al. (2015) concluded that the seismicity was induced by the hydraulic fracturing operations. Key evidence:

- Stage-by-stage operational correlation between fracturing stages and seismicity
- Temporal coincidence during active pumping
- No prior seismicity in the immediate area
- Seismicity consistent with communication to a pre-existing basement fault

The Poland Township case was one of the first published examples of hydraulic-fracturing-induced seismicity in Ohio and contributed to regulatory developments in the state.

## Scientific Controversy

The attribution is broadly accepted. The case is notable for its regulatory impact: it led Ohio to adopt specific traffic-light protocols for hydraulic fracturing operations, the first such regulation in the United States.
