## Geological Setting

The Pohang Enhanced Geothermal System (EGS) project was located in the southeastern Korean Peninsula, in a region characterized by a compressional tectonic stress regime with NE-SW oriented maximum horizontal stress. The area had experienced very low levels of natural seismicity prior to project operations — fewer than a handful of M > 2.0 events were recorded in the region during the 15 years of instrumental monitoring preceding the project. The geothermal target was crystalline basement rock at approximately 4–5 km depth, which was stimulated by high-pressure fluid injection to create a permeable fracture network.

## Industrial Operations

The Pohang EGS project involved two deep wells (PX-1 and PX-2) drilled to approximately 4.2 km depth into granitic basement. The project aimed to stimulate natural fractures by injecting water at pressures sufficient to cause hydraulic shearing, creating permeability for a closed-loop geothermal heat exchange system. Multiple stimulation stages were conducted across both wells between 2016 and 2017, involving tens of thousands of cubic meters of injected fluid. Seismic monitoring was in place throughout operations, and several induced microseismic events were detected during and after stimulation stages.

## Earthquake Sequence

Following the second stimulation well's injection phases, a foreshock sequence began, culminating in a M5.5 mainshock on November 15, 2017. The mainshock occurred approximately 0.6 km from the surface projection of the injection well. The earthquake caused significant damage to buildings in Pohang — notably affecting thousands of residents in prefabricated housing — and injured over 100 people. An aftershock sequence followed, including multiple events above M3. The mainshock occurred on a previously unmapped fault structure in the basement.

## Attribution in the Published Literature

A government-commissioned independent expert panel concluded in 2019 that the Pohang earthquake was triggered by the EGS project. This conclusion was subsequently supported by multiple independent peer-reviewed studies. Key evidence cited includes:

- The 0.6 km distance between the mainshock hypocenter and the nearest injection well
- Temporal correlation between stimulation stages and onset of anomalous seismicity
- Complete absence of M > 2.0 events in the 15-year pre-project monitoring record
- Hypocenters of the foreshock sequence clustering within the depth range of the stimulated interval
- Focal mechanism solutions indicating reverse faulting consistent with the regional compressive stress regime
- Coulomb stress modeling suggesting injection-induced pore pressure increase could have reactivated a pre-existing unmapped basement fault

Pressure diffusion modeling was subsequently conducted in several studies to connect injection history to observed seismicity timing and location.

## Scientific Controversy

The Pohang case is notable for the policy and legal consequences of the attribution: the government panel's conclusion contributed to the permanent shutdown of the project and subsequent civil litigation. While the scientific community broadly accepts a causal link between EGS stimulation and the mainshock, debates exist regarding:

1. Whether the mainshock was a direct hydraulic fracturing event or was triggered by a more complex interaction involving aseismic slip and stress transfer
2. The relative roles of PX-1 versus PX-2 injection in contributing to the final mainshock
3. Whether the traffic-light protocol used during operations was adequate given the pre-existing fault condition

The Pohang case raised international awareness about seismic hazard from EGS projects and influenced geothermal regulatory frameworks in several countries.

## Confidence Scale Discrepancy (Known Issue)

The POHANG-T1-Q1 item has a documented discrepancy between source materials: the slide deck used in course presentations labels the reference confidence as 2 (Plausible), while the dataset draft table assigns it 1 (Weakly suggestive). The canonical Tier N → Ref_conf N mapping has been adopted as the standard; this assigns Ref_conf = 1 to Tier 1 items. The discrepancy is flagged in `annotation_notes` for POHANG-T1-Q1 and should be resolved through domain expert adjudication in future versions.

## Dataset Notes

The POHANG case contributes three items spanning evidence tiers 1–3. A Tier 4 item (with pressure diffusion modeling) is planned for future dataset expansion but is not included in the current 10-item draft. The Pohang case is the only geothermal operation in the current dataset and provides a contrast with the wastewater disposal cases (PRAGUE, RATON) in terms of operation type, depth of target, and regulatory context.
