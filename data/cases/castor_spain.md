## Geological Setting

The Castor underground gas storage facility was located offshore in the Mediterranean Sea near the Castellón coast of Spain. The storage reservoir was a depleted offshore oil field in Mesozoic carbonate rocks at approximately 1.7–1.8 km depth below the seabed. The region sits within a seismically active Mediterranean tectonic setting characterized by normal faulting in an extensional stress regime. Background seismicity in the area is moderate.

## Industrial Operations

The Castor facility was designed to store natural gas in the depleted Amposta oil field for use as a strategic reserve. Gas injection operations began in June 2013. Approximately 50 million standard cubic meters of gas had been injected when the seismicity prompted operational suspension.

## Earthquake Sequence

Shortly after gas injection operations commenced in mid-2013, a seismicity sequence developed in the area of the storage field. The sequence escalated rapidly, culminating in multiple events exceeding M4, with the largest reaching M4.1 in late September 2013. The sequence included approximately 1,000 events in total. The largest felt events caused public concern in coastal communities and prompted the Spanish government to suspend operations permanently.

## Attribution in the Published Literature

González et al. (2013) and Gaite et al. (2016) concluded that the seismicity was induced by gas injection into the storage reservoir. Key evidence:

- Temporal coincidence of seismicity onset with gas injection commencement
- Spatial coincidence of seismicity with the storage field footprint
- Operational-status-correlated seismicity rate (escalation during injection, decline after suspension)
- Hypocenters consistent with the reservoir and bounding fault depth
- Normal-faulting focal mechanisms consistent with the regional extensional stress regime
- Compatibility of the seismicity with reactivation of faults bounding the depleted reservoir structure

## Scientific Controversy

The attribution is broadly accepted. The Castor case highlights the seismic risk associated with underground gas storage in depleted fields, particularly in tectonically active regions. The Spanish government ultimately abandoned the facility and provided financial compensation. A larger historical earthquake in the area (M6.7 in 1748) on a nearby fault raised concerns about the potential for stress triggering of larger structures.
