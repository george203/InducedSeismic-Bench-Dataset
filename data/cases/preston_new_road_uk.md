## Geological Setting

The Preston New Road site is located in Lancashire, northwestern England, near the earlier Preese Hall hydraulic fracturing site. The target formation is the Bowland Shale at approximately 2.5–3 km depth. The tectonic setting involves Carboniferous fault systems in a transpressional stress regime. The area has some background seismicity associated with regional fault structures.

## Industrial Operations

Cuadrilla Resources conducted hydraulic fracturing at the Preston New Road-1z well beginning in October 2018, after several years of regulatory review following the Preese Hall moratorium. The well targeted the Bowland Shale for shale gas exploration.

## Earthquake Sequence

Hydraulic fracturing operations produced a sustained sequence of felt seismicity throughout the fracturing program in 2018–2019. Multiple events exceeded the traffic-light protocol thresholds, leading to repeated operational pauses. The largest event reached M2.9 on August 26, 2019. The persistent felt seismicity despite operational modifications ultimately led the UK government to impose an indefinite moratorium on hydraulic fracturing in England in November 2019.

## Attribution in the Published Literature

The British Geological Survey and Oil and Gas Authority documented the seismicity and its operational correlations. Published analyses confirmed:

- Stage-by-stage operational correlation between fracturing activity and seismicity
- Seismicity consistent with activation of pre-existing fault structures at fracturing depth
- Focal mechanisms consistent with the regional stress field
- Depth consistency with the Bowland Shale interval and adjacent faults

The Preston New Road case, coming seven years after Preese Hall, demonstrated that the seismic risk from shale hydraulic fracturing in the Bowland Basin was not easily mitigated by operational modifications alone.

## Scientific Controversy

The attribution of the seismicity to fracturing operations is not disputed. The scientific and policy debate concerns the appropriate level of seismic risk tolerance for shale gas operations and whether the UK traffic-light protocol thresholds are appropriate.
