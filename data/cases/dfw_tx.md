## Geological Setting

The Dallas-Fort Worth earthquake swarm occurred in the Tarrant County area of north Texas, in the vicinity of the Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport. The region is underlain by Paleozoic and Mesozoic sedimentary rocks overlying Precambrian crystalline basement. The area had no documented natural seismicity prior to the swarm. The geological setting is within the Fort Worth Basin.

## Industrial Operations

A single Class II wastewater disposal well at the Dallas-Fort Worth Airport began operations in September 2008, injecting produced water from nearby Barnett Shale gas production into a deep saline formation (the Ellenburger Group) at approximately 4 km depth. The Ellenburger is equivalent to the Arbuckle Group in Oklahoma and similarly overlies crystalline basement in this area.

## Earthquake Sequence

The DFW swarm began approximately two months after injection commenced at the airport disposal well, in November 2008. The swarm included approximately 170 earthquakes through mid-2009, with the largest reaching M3.3. The swarm was felt by DFW airport personnel and nearby residents. The tight spatial association with the single disposal well and the two-month onset lag consistent with pressure diffusion timescales made this a particularly clear case study.

## Attribution in the Published Literature

Frohlich et al. (2011) and Frohlich (2012) concluded that the swarm was induced by the single airport disposal well. Key evidence:

- Temporal onset approximately two months after injection commencement, consistent with pressure diffusion to the nearest fault
- Spatial proximity (within 1–2 km) of the swarm to the single disposal well
- No prior seismicity in the area
- Injection-rate-correlated seismicity rate
- Hypocentral depths at Ellenburger/basement level
- Strike-slip focal mechanisms consistent with the regional stress field

The single-well constraint, combined with the known injection start date and the two-month onset lag, makes DFW a particularly well-documented single-source case.

## Scientific Controversy

The DFW case is not significantly contested. The attribution to the single airport disposal well is broadly accepted. The case is notable for occurring in a major urban and critical infrastructure setting.
